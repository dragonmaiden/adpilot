# OPERATING-PLAYBOOK.md

Use this playbook to make consistent, commercially useful Meta ads judgments.

## Diagnostic order

Always reason in this order:
1. Economics
2. Signal quality
3. Funnel bottleneck
4. Structure quality
5. Creative quality

## Economics first

Before giving budget or scaling guidance, check or estimate:
- average order value
- gross margin
- contribution margin
- break-even CPA
- target CPA
- break-even ROAS
- target ROAS
- refund pressure
- cash or inventory constraints if known

If economics are weak, scaling is not the answer.

## Signal quality

Reduce confidence when:
- sample size is weak
- conversion count is low
- attribution is distorted
- refunds or pending recovery materially change the picture
- backend and platform data disagree
- COGS coverage is incomplete

## Funnel bottleneck hints

- low CTR: weak hook or wrong message
- high CTR, weak conversion: landing mismatch, offer weakness, or audience quality issue
- acceptable ROAS, weak profit: economics or refunds problem
- rising CPA with falling CTR: fatigue or decaying creative quality
- good conversion rate but low delivery: budget, bid, or structure problem

## Budget decision buckets

Classify each entity into one of:
- scale aggressively
- scale cautiously
- hold and observe
- fix before scaling
- reduce budget
- pause / kill

Default to caution when evidence is mixed.

## Structure rules

Prefer simplicity when possible.

Bad signs:
- too many campaigns with tiny spend
- duplicated creatives across entities
- audience overlap
- overfunded retargeting
- fragmented learning

## Creative rules

Assume creative may be the bottleneck until ruled out.

Watch for:
- falling CTR
- higher CPM with weaker engagement
- winner concentration
- creative fatigue
- weak angle diversity

Never say "test more creatives" without naming the hypothesis.

## Output standard

For operator answers, default to:
- what is happening
- biggest issue
- biggest opportunity
- recommended action
- confidence
- caveats
